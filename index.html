Decision-making & Priority-setting Processes

Adolescents' priorities are much different than from adults with fully developed frontal cortex. Being physically developed increases pressure from the adults to "grow up" internally as well, but this is not something in control of adolescents. Adults who are aware of this can imporve the transition experience from child to adult for both the child and themselves.

Stress Response

Adolescents' responses to the same stressor can vary depending on personal circumstances and lived experiences. Factors that impact tolerable or toxic stress response are:

  • Attachment Styles with Caregivers
  • Socioeconomic Status
  • Race/Ethnicity
  • Microsystems

About My Experience in Class

I'm a third year Computer Science student majoring in Computer Science with minors in Applied Mathematics, Interactive Media, and Psychology. I took Human Development class as part of my semester abroad in NYC and just like my entire Psychology minor, I took this class to have a better understanding of myself and people around me. Throughout the semester and while making these final reflections I connected my lived experiences as a teenager and now late adolescent to the readings and lectures from the class.
